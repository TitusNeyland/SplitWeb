Section 3

Information we collect.

In short: We collect account info, what you're splitting, how you pay, and a small amount of technical data needed to run the app. We spell out every category below.

3.1 Account & profile data

  • Full name, username, and email address
  • Phone number (used to match with friends and for account recovery)
  • Password — we never store passwords in plain text. Authentication is handled by Firebase Authentication, which stores a salted scrypt-based hash.
  • Profile photo, if you upload one (stored in Firebase Storage)
  • Date your account was created
  • Sign-in method (email + password, Sign in with Apple, or Sign in with Google)

3.2 Financial & payment data

  • Payment method metadata — card brand, last 4 digits, and expiration month/year. Your full card number is tokenized by Stripe and never reaches our servers.
  • Billing address, if we're required to collect it for tax purposes
  • Transaction history inside Kilo (amounts, dates, which split, which members)
  • Your share allocation for each split
  • Subscription plan status (Free / Kilo Plus) and any Stripe billing history for your subscription
  • If you're the organizer of a split, Stripe may share limited dispute or chargeback evidence with us if a member disputes a charge

3.3 Device & usage data

  • Device model, operating system and version, app version
  • IP address, and a coarse location derived from your IP (country / region — not GPS)
  • Language and timezone
  • Crash logs and performance metrics
  • In-app events (screens viewed, features used) — used to improve the product
  • Push notification token (APNs on iOS, FCM on Android)

3.4 Content you create in Kilo

  • Splits and subscriptions you add (service name, cost, billing cycle, custom labels, notes)
  • Receipt images you scan — stored encrypted in Firebase Storage. We run optical character recognition (OCR) on them to pre-fill line items. Images are retained for [RETENTION DAYS, e.g. 90 days] after the associated split is closed, then deleted.
  • OCR output (extracted text from your receipts)
  • Messages or notes you attach to a split (if applicable)

3.5 Contacts

Kilo handles your address book carefully. We only access your contacts after you grant explicit OS permission. We never upload raw phone numbers, names, or other contact details to our servers.

When you tap "Find friends on Kilo," the app:

  1. Reads phone numbers from your device after you grant permission
  2. Normalizes them to E.164 format (e.g., +15551234567)
  3. Hashes each number locally using SHA-256
  4. Sends only the hashes to our servers to check for matches with existing Kilo users
  5. Deletes any hash that didn't match within 24 hours
  6. Stores only hashes for contacts you explicitly choose to invite (so we don't show invite suggestions you already dismissed)

Your contacts' raw names and phone numbers never leave your device.

3.6 Camera & photo library

  • Camera: used only when you tap "Scan receipt." Captured images are processed as described in §3.4.
  • Photo library: used only when you choose a profile photo, or select a receipt from your library instead of scanning one.
  • Microphone: Kilo does not use the microphone.

3.7 Biometric data (Face ID / Touch ID / fingerprint)

If you enable biometric unlock, your fingerprint or face scan never leaves your device. Kilo does not see, collect, or store biometric data. We only receive a yes/no signal from your operating system indicating whether authentication succeeded.

3.8 Third-party sign-in

  • Sign in with Apple: We receive your name (if you choose to share it) and either your real email address or Apple's relay email. We do not receive any other information from Apple.
  • Sign in with Google: We receive your name, email address, Google account ID, and profile photo URL. We do not request access to Gmail, Drive, Calendar, or any other Google data.

Section 4

How we collect it.

In short: Most of it comes from you directly. Some is automatic (device info). A small amount comes from partners like Stripe, Apple, and Google.
  • Directly from you — when you sign up, edit your profile, add a split, or contact support.
  • Automatically — device, usage, and crash data collected as you use the app.
  • From third parties — Stripe (payment status), Apple (if you use Sign in with Apple), Google (if you use Sign in with Google), and push-notification providers (delivery status).
  • From other users — if a friend invites you to Kilo, their device sends us a hashed version of your phone number so we can match you up when you join. If you never join, the hash is deleted after [30 days] of inactivity.

Section 9

How we protect your data.

In short: Encryption in transit and at rest, strict access controls, and a documented breach-response plan.
  • In transit: All connections between your device and our servers are encrypted with TLS 1.2 or higher.
  • At rest: Firebase Firestore and Firebase Storage encrypt data at rest by default using AES-256.
  • Authentication: Passwords are hashed (scrypt) by Firebase Authentication and never stored or transmitted in plain text.
  • Payments: Stripe is PCI-DSS Level 1 certified. Full card numbers never reach our servers.
  • Access controls: Internal access to production data is limited to a small set of employees, logged, and requires multi-factor authentication.
  • Optional in-app protections: You can enable biometric unlock and/or a 6-digit PIN inside the app.
  • Incident response: If a data breach affects your Personal Data, we will notify affected users and the appropriate supervisory authority without undue delay — within 72 hours where required by GDPR.

No security program is perfect. We don't claim "military-grade" or "bank-level" security; we implement industry-standard safeguards and continuously improve them.

Section 13

Automated decisions & AI.

In short: We use ML for receipt OCR and fraud scoring, but a real person reviews any consequential decision.

We use machine learning in two places:

  • Receipt OCR — to turn a photo of a receipt into editable line items. You can always edit the results.
  • Fraud & risk scoring — Stripe Radar evaluates payment attempts to detect fraud. A high-risk score may result in a payment being declined.

We do not make decisions that have a legal or similarly significant effect on you solely by automated means. If a payment is declined for risk reasons, you can contact support and we will review.

Section 15

Do Not Track & Global Privacy Control.

In short: We honor Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals from your browser.

Browsers can send a Do Not Track (DNT) signal. There is no industry consensus on how to interpret DNT, so we do not change our practices in response to it.

We do honor Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals as a valid opt-out of the "sale" or "sharing" of Personal Information under CCPA/CPRA. If our site detects a GPC signal from your browser, we treat it as an opt-out request for that browser / device.
